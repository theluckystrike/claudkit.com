About ClaudKit

The story behind the toolkit and the network of developer resources we are building.

Our Mission

ClaudKit was created to solve a specific problem: developers building AI-powered applications need production-quality code examples and patterns, not simplified tutorials that fall apart under real-world conditions. The gap between a working demo and a production system is vast in AI development, and most available resources focus exclusively on the demo side. We focus on what comes after the demo works.

Every code snippet we publish has been tested against live AI APIs under realistic conditions. Our architecture patterns address the specific challenges of AI applications: non-deterministic outputs, rate limiting, cost management, quality evaluation, and the continuous prompt maintenance that AI features require after launch. We document the edge cases, failure modes, and operational concerns that tutorials typically omit because they are not relevant to a five-minute demo.

We support multiple programming languages and frameworks because we believe developers should be able to build AI applications in whatever stack they are already productive with. Our code examples are available in Python, TypeScript, Go, and Rust, with consistent patterns across all languages so you can apply what you learn regardless of your preferred environment. We also maintain framework-specific guides for FastAPI, Next.js, and other popular choices for AI application backends.

Part of a Larger Network

ClaudKit is part of a network of specialized AI and ML resources designed to cover the full breadth of the ecosystem. Our flagship resource, zovo.one, serves as the comprehensive hub for discovering AI tools and platforms. ClaudKit focuses specifically on the developer experience of building with AI, complementing the broader discovery and evaluation that zovo.one provides.
